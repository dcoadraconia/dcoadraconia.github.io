Now, the young Orange Leaf Dragon stood at the top of the basement steps, looking down into the dark, mildewy basement chamber lit by the dim sunlight shining through the basement window-wells.

The windows and walls were covered in an overgrowth of twisted green vines. The basement was also coated in a thin layer of moss, which almost seemed to produce a dull green glow.

Ever since she was small, this young dragon had nightmares about this place. There was something so... unsettling about the walls, the vines, the cramped room...

It had always been incredibly unsettling, yet at the same time... There was an odd sense of familiarity.
Almost like she'd spent a very long time down here in the past.

But that was COMPLETELY not true. She HATED the basement! She'd never spend any more time here than she ABSOLUTELY had to.

Standing here now, the nightmares were beginning to flash through her mind...
The vines, reaching out toward her...
The creepy whispers, telling her that she couldn't escape...
The eerie green glow, the constant chill in the air...

And worst of all of the recurring nightmares, the dark green snake that would slither out from the vines, toward her while she stood paralyzed in the basement, unable to run away...
... It would bite into her leg, injecting its poison into her until she collapsed, and then slowly drag her into the vines, to become part of their tangled mass...

...

She HATED snakes, she HATED the nightmares, and she ABSOLUTELY HATED the basement.

The dragon turned around to face the basement door. She wanted to leave... She really wanted to leave, RIGHT NOW.

But her parents had said to stay there... They had said it was really dangerous to leave...
And she was barely strong enough to move the basement door on her own, anyway.

She was brave enough to stay down here for a little while! It's not like she's a "fraidy-dragon" or anything like that...

And surely, her mother and father wouldn't keep her waiting for long...
